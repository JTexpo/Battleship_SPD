How to use this app
When you load the webpage, you'll encounter two visuals. The Battleship game board on the left, and a heatmap of the AI's moves on the right.

Click the water tiles to fire at the AI's battleships. If you hit a battleship, you get to take your turn again. Continue trading fire until there is a winner indicated by all of the shits being hit.

Both players have the following ships:
  • 1 ship of size 5x1
  • 3 ships of size 3x1
  • 1 ship of size 2x1

Once the AI discovers all the ships, the heatmap turns black and white. You are allowed you to continue guessing even after the game is over, incase you are wondering where the other ships are.

When the game is over click the "Reset" button to get a new board.

What is Spacial Density Probability?
Spacial Density Probability (SPD) in statistics involves evaluating possible layouts for given use-cases and creating a density graph.

In the case of Battleship, this concept helps us identify the most and least popular segments of the map.

For instance, at the beginning of the game, the center of the map is often the most dense area. This is because all ship orientations have a higher probability of having at least one part within the middle four tiles.

By understanding SPD, we can strategically predict optimal moves for Battleship (the most dense tiles), enhancing the decision-making process.
